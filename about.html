ABOUT ME

I am a senior at Bennington College, where I study mathematics and ceramics. My academic work spans statistics, mathematical modeling, applied mathematics, and coding, with experience in R and Python. I enjoy exploring complex data sets and building models that provide insight into real world problems.

Growing up in Rhode Island, I have always felt a deep connection to the coastal environment, and this influences much of my work. Whether through data-driven research on marine ecosystems or ceramic forms that abstract the textures and rhythms of the ocean, I strive to bridge science and art in meaningful ways. My projects often explore environmental themes, blending analytical rigor with creative expression to better understand and represent the natural world.
